Liferecord — a warm, simple way for people to record their life story and the lessons they've learned, 
before those memories are lost.

Most tools for capturing a life story assume the writer can type comfortably and structure a narrative on their own.
Liferecord assumes the opposite: large text, minimal friction, and 
voice as a first-class input — because many of the people who most need this can't type easily,
and speech-to-text often mishears them. AI in this app exists specifically to solve that:
it cleans up garbled voice transcriptions, helps a writer continue when they're stuck,
and can reorganize a rambling, out-of-order retelling into chronological order — all while 
preserving the person's own words and voice.

Key features:
- Free-form life stories or guided reflective questions ("lessons learned"), public or private
- Voice dictation with AI cleanup for mis-transcribed speech
- AI writing assistant and AI chronological-order rewriting for a single story
- A free-form family tree — connect relatives with custom relationship labels,
- discover possible matches across other users' public trees
- PDF export of stories and family trees
- Full UI in 6 languages (English, French, Spanish, German, Chinese, Hungarian)
- plus on-demand AI translation of public stories
- Light/dark/system theme, all preferences synced to the account

Try it live: https://frontend-nine-sable-72.vercel.app

Built with: Next.js 16 + Chakra UI, Express + SQLite, Groq (Llama 3.3) for AI features, DeepL for translation, 
Resend for email.
